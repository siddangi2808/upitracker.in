The World's First UPI Expansion Tracker

We track every international bank, payment network, and merchant that accepts UPI outside India — so 32 million Indian travelers and the global diaspora always know where India pays.

🎯

Our Purpose

UPI is expanding globally at an unprecedented pace. Yet no single public source tracks where it works, which banks support it, or which merchants accept it. UPI Global Tracker fills that gap — free, open, and transparent.

🌍

Who It's For

Indian travelers abroad, the NRI diaspora, fintech researchers, journalists, travel apps, banks expanding internationally, and policymakers monitoring India's fintech soft power globally.

🔄

How We Stay Current

Data is sourced from NPCI International press releases, RBI circulars, and official MoU announcements — verified by our community of contributors before publishing.

🔓

Fully Public

This is a public interest resource. All tracker data is free to browse. A developer API is available (coming soon) for apps wishing to integrate UPI acceptance data into their products.

🤝

Community-First

Updates, corrections, and additions are submitted by the community — verified travelers, fintech professionals, NRI banking users, and local correspondents across 20+ countries.

📡

No Affiliation

We are not affiliated with NPCI, NIPL, RBI, or any bank. This is an independent tracker built in the public interest. We do not receive data fees or payments for listings.

Powered by the Community

UPI Global Tracker relies on a growing network of contributors — travelers, fintech researchers, bank employees, and journalists — who report what they see on the ground.

When a new country goes live, when a merchant stops accepting UPI, or when a bank launches cross-border support, our community is the first to know. You can be part of that.

How to contribute
1
